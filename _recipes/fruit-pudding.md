### Ingredients

- 1 litre milk
- china-grass, as needed
- sugar, to taste
- mixed fruit, chopped
- optional fruits: apple, banana, orange, mosambi, mango

### Directions

1. Soak the china-grass in a bowl of normal water. Use enough water to fully submerge it, pressing it down gently with your hands. Let it soak for at least 10 minutes, and up to 45 minutes if needed.
2. Heat the milk in a pan and bring it to a boil.
3. Add the soaked china-grass to the hot milk and keep stirring on low heat until it fully dissolves into the milk and you can no longer see any strands. This usually takes around 5 minutes.
4. Add sugar to taste and cook for another 1-2 minutes until dissolved.
5. Remove the milk from the stove. The china-grass will begin to set quickly as it cools, so keep the fruit ready in advance.
6. Chop the fruit you want to use. If any fruit is too sour, sprinkle a little sugar over it and let it sit briefly.
7. When the milk mixture has cooled slightly but is still pourable, add the fruit and mix well.
8. Pour into a serving bowl or individual bowls.
9. Refrigerate until set. Do not freeze.
10. Serve chilled once the pudding is cool and nicely set.

### Notes

- This makes a white-colored pudding because the china-grass mixture has no added color.
- If too much china-grass is added, the pudding will set very fast and become too firm.
- A simple first version is best: make the plain fruit pudding before trying flavored jelly or crystal additions.
